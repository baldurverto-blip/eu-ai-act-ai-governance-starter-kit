## Overview

This starter kit provides a practical first layer of AI documentation and governance for EU teams already using AI in everyday business operations. The goal is to help a team move from undocumented AI usage to a credible, editable baseline with named ownership, lightweight controls, and repeatable review.

**What this kit is:**
- A small-team governance documentation baseline
- Editable templates adaptable to your team's context
- A practical starting point for organizing AI use before it becomes harder to govern
- Material that can support later EU AI Act review without claiming to complete it

**What this kit is NOT:**
- Legal advice
- Guaranteed compliance with the EU AI Act
- A compliance certification
- A bespoke audit
- A substitute for specialist review for high-risk or heavily regulated use cases

## Credibility and approach

This product is intentionally specific about scope.

- It focuses on ordinary business AI usage such as drafting, support, research, coding assistance, and internal operations.
- It is built for teams that need usable documentation rather than a large implementation project.
- It avoids promising legal outcomes that a static toolkit cannot honestly deliver.

Why this approach is credible:

- buyers can inspect concrete files
- the deliverables cover multiple parts of the workflow, not just policy language
- the fit boundaries are explicit
- the tone is operational rather than promotional

## Deliverables

### Core governance documents

| File | What it does | Why it matters |
|------|--------------|----------------|
| `AI-usage-policy.md` | Sets internal rules for approved use, prohibited behavior, confidentiality boundaries, and escalation triggers. | Gives the team one place to define acceptable AI use instead of letting norms develop informally. |
| `ai-systems-register-template.md` | Tracks AI tools, use cases, owners, data categories, review dates, and controls. | Creates the inventory that most teams need before they can govern anything consistently. |
| `internal-review-sop.md` | Defines human review steps for AI-assisted output before external use or important decisions. | Helps teams reduce risk from unchecked AI output and assign review responsibility. |
| `incident-log-template.md` | Logs hallucinations, misuse, policy breaches, data exposure concerns, and corrective actions. | Builds a basic evidence trail and remediation record when something goes wrong. |
| `vendor-review-checklist.md` | Supports lightweight diligence for AI vendors, providers, and embedded assistants. | Makes vendor approval more disciplined without turning it into a heavyweight procurement process. |

## Why the pack feels substantial

The value is not "five templates" in the abstract. The value is that the kit covers the main documentation jobs a small team usually needs first:

1. Define acceptable use and escalation boundaries
2. Inventory tools and use cases
3. Add a human review checkpoint where needed
4. Record incidents and remediation
5. Capture lightweight vendor diligence

Together, these files form a compact governance baseline that a small team can adapt quickly and keep current over time.

## First-week rollout

1. Read through all templates and confirm the intended owners
2. Adapt the AI Usage Policy to your actual approved-use boundaries
3. Populate the AI Systems Register with current tools and real use cases
4. Decide which outputs require human review before external use
5. Publish the incident log and vendor checklist for ongoing use

For many teams, this is a 60-90 minute first pass rather than a full project.

---

## Ongoing use

- Update the register when new AI tools or use cases are introduced
- Review policy language quarterly or when tool usage changes materially
- Log incidents as they occur
- Re-run vendor review when adopting new providers or materially changing usage
