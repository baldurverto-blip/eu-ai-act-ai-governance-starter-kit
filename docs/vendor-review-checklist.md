# Vendor AI Review Checklist

> **Purpose:** Give a small team a usable due-diligence checklist before adopting or expanding an AI vendor or tool.
> **Status:** Editable starter checklist.
> **Audience:** Small EU teams buying or approving mainstream AI tools for ordinary business workflows.
> **Important:** This checklist supports internal decision-making. It does not replace legal, security, procurement, privacy, or technical review where those are needed.

## When to use this checklist

Use this checklist before:

- adopting a new AI tool for work
- expanding an existing tool into a new department or data type
- connecting an AI tool to internal systems or automation
- approving a vendor for workflows involving personal data, customer content, or confidential information

You do not need enterprise procurement bureaucracy to be disciplined. You do need a repeatable set of questions.

## What this checklist is designed to do

It helps a small team answer:

- Is this a real, supportable vendor?
- What data will the vendor receive?
- Are the terms and settings compatible with our intended use?
- Are we relying on vague assumptions about privacy, training, or retention?
- What limits or controls do we need before use?

## Suggested workflow

1. Requestor completes the first pass.
2. Owner or manager reviews the business purpose and fit.
3. Security, ops, or privacy owner reviews the data and vendor risk questions.
4. Decision is recorded as approve, approve with conditions, defer, or reject.
5. If approved, the tool is added to the AI systems register with an owner and review date.

Examples of approval conditions:

- no personal or client data
- second-person review required before external use
- no API or system integration until a further review
- re-review in 30 or 60 days after real usage begins

## Example completed summary

### Example use case

Marketing wants to use an AI writing tool for blog outlines, campaign drafts, and repurposing existing public content.

### Example assessment

| Area | Assessment | Notes / rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Vendor reliability | Low concern | Established vendor with documentation, support, and stable commercial presence |
| Privacy and data handling | Medium concern | Public content is fine, but customer case-study drafts must stay out unless separately approved |
| Security and access | Low concern | Workspace admin controls available and no deep integrations required initially |
| Output and workflow risk | Medium concern | Public output may overstate claims; manager review required before publication |
| Commercial practicality | Low concern | Low subscription cost and clear use case |
| Overall assessment | Medium concern | Approve with conditions |

### Example approval conditions

- Use only for internal drafting and public marketing drafts.
- Do not upload customer-confidential material.
- Manager review required before publication.
- Add the tool to the AI systems register and review again in 60 days.

## Common mistakes when reviewing AI vendors

- approving the tool because the brand is familiar without checking the actual workflow
- assuming "enterprise" or "team" branding means the data setup is automatically acceptable
- reviewing the vendor but not the intended use case
- forgetting to set conditions and ownership
- treating one-time approval as permanent

## Implementation notes for buyers

For a small team, this checklist should usually take 15-30 minutes for a straightforward tool and longer only where the workflow is sensitive.

That is enough rigor to improve buying discipline without creating enterprise-style procurement drag.
