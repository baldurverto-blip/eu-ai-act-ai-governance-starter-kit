# AI Usage Policy Template

> **Purpose:** Internal policy governing employee use of AI tools in business operations.
> **Status:** Starter template — adapt to your team's context.
> **Not legal advice.** Review with legal counsel for your jurisdiction.

---

## 1. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and team members who use AI tools in any business context — including but not limited to:

- Drafting and editing documents
- Research and summarization
- Customer support assistance
- Internal operations and process automation
- Coding and technical workflows

## 2. Approved Use

The following uses are permitted without prior approval:

- **Productivity tools:** AI-assisted writing, summarization, translation, and drafting within approved platforms
- **Research:** AI-assisted information gathering, analysis, and synthesis for internal use
- **Internal communications:** AI-assisted email drafting, meeting notes, and internal documentation
- **Coding assistance:** AI code completion, review suggestions, and technical debugging

## 3. Prohibited Use

The following uses are prohibited:

- **Customer-facing output without review:** Publishing or sending AI-generated content directly to customers, candidates, or external stakeholders without human review
- **Confidential data input:** Uploading legally privileged, highly sensitive, or personally identifiable information (PII) to external AI tools without explicit approval
- **High-risk decisions:** Using AI to make hiring, firing, credit, healthcare, or legal decisions without human oversight
- **Disallowed tools:** Using AI tools or services not on the approved vendor list

## 4. Confidentiality Boundaries

- **Internal-only data:** Do not input internal strategies, roadmaps, financials, or employee data into external AI tools unless approved by your manager.
- **Customer data:** Customer information may only be processed through AI tools with explicit customer consent or under approved data processing agreements.
- **Third-party data:** Do not input proprietary information belonging to clients, partners, or other third parties into AI tools without authorization.

## 5. Review Requirements

AI-assisted output **must** be reviewed by a human before it:

- Reaches customers or external stakeholders
- Is used in product, marketing, or public-facing materials
- Informs business decisions with material impact
- Is stored as organizational knowledge or policy

The reviewing person must understand the content and take responsibility for its accuracy.

## 6. Escalation Triggers

Escalate to your manager and the security owner immediately if:

- An AI tool produces output that appears fabricated, misleading, or harmful
- A data exposure or confidentiality breach is suspected
- An AI tool vendor experiences a security incident or data breach
- The tool is used for a use case not covered by this policy

## 7. AI Tools Approved for Use

| Tool Category | Approved Tools |
|---------------|----------------|
| Writing & Editing | ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Notion AI, Microsoft Copilot |
| Research & Analysis | Perplexity, Gemini, ChatGPT |
| Coding | GitHub Copilot, Cursor, Claude Code |
| Internal Ops | Notion AI, Microsoft Copilot, Google Gemini |

> **Note:** This list is reviewed quarterly. New tools require security owner approval before use.

## 8. Training & Attestation

All team members must:

1. Read this policy
2. Complete the training acknowledgment form
3. Report any policy questions or concerns to their manager

## 9. Exceptions

Any exception to this policy requires written approval from your manager and the security owner.
