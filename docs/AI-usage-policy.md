# AI Usage Policy Template

> **Purpose:** Internal policy governing employee use of AI tools in business operations.
> **Status:** Starter template — adapt to your team's context.
> **Not legal advice.** Review with legal counsel for your jurisdiction and specific use cases.
> **Audience:** Small EU teams (5–100 people) using AI for ordinary business tasks.

## Why your team needs this

Without a clear policy, AI use becomes a grey area where individual judgment varies wildly. One person inputs customer data; another doesn't. One team reviews everything; another assumes "it's just a draft." A written policy creates shared understanding and gives your team a defensible baseline if questions arise later.

This template covers the five areas small teams typically struggle with:
1. What is actually permitted
2. What is prohibited
3. Where confidentiality boundaries exist
4. When human review is required
5. What triggers escalation

---

## 1. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and team members who use AI tools in any business context — including but not limited to:

- Drafting and editing documents, emails, and proposals
- Research, summarization, and information synthesis
- Customer support assistance and response drafting
- Internal operations and process automation
- Coding, technical documentation, and debugging
- Marketing copy, social media, and content creation

**Who owns this:** The designated AI governance owner (often ops lead, security owner, or compliance lead) is responsible for maintaining this policy and ensuring team acknowledgment.

## 2. Approved Use

The following uses are permitted without prior approval, provided they follow confidentiality and review requirements elsewhere in this policy:

- **Productivity tools:** AI-assisted writing, summarization, translation, and drafting within approved platforms
- **Research:** AI-assisted information gathering, analysis, and synthesis for internal use
- **Internal communications:** AI-assisted email drafting, meeting notes, and internal documentation
- **Coding assistance:** AI code completion, review suggestions, and technical debugging
- **Content creation:** Marketing copy, social media drafts, and blog outlines (subject to human review before publication)

> **Tip:** If your team uses specific tools not listed here, add them to Section 7. Review the list quarterly as the AI tool landscape evolves quickly.

## 3. Prohibited Use

The following uses are prohibited to protect your team, customers, and business:

- **Customer-facing output without review:** Publishing or sending AI-generated content directly to customers, candidates, or external stakeholders without human review — this is the most common failure point for small teams
- **Confidential data input:** Uploading legally privileged, highly sensitive, or personally identifiable information (PII) to external AI tools without explicit approval from your manager or security owner
- **High-risk decisions:** Using AI to make hiring, firing, credit, healthcare, or legal decisions without human oversight and appropriate specialist review
- **Disallowed tools:** Using AI tools or services not on the approved vendor list (see Section 7)
- **Copying proprietary content:** Entering trade secrets, proprietary algorithms, or confidential client information into AI tools without authorization

> **Why this matters for the EU AI Act:** While your team may not be deploying high-risk AI systems, documenting clear boundaries creates a defensible record if your AI use evolves or if questions arise from partners or regulators.

## 4. Confidentiality Boundaries

This section addresses the reality that small teams often blur the line between "internal" and "external" data. Be specific about what your team actually handles:

- **Internal-only data:** Do not input internal strategies, roadmaps, financials, salary information, or employee personal data into external AI tools unless approved by your manager. This includes:
  - Business plans and strategy documents
  - Financial projections and pricing models
  - Employee performance reviews and personnel files
- **Customer data:** Customer information (names, emails, project details, contracts) may only be processed through AI tools with explicit customer consent, an approved data processing agreement, or under your existing GDPR framework.
- **Third-party data:** Do not input proprietary information belonging to clients, partners, or other third parties into AI tools without written authorization.
- **Special category data:** Health data, biometric data, political opinions, religious beliefs, trade union membership, and sexual orientation must never be input to external AI tools.

> **Quick test:** If you would not email this data to a stranger, do not input it to an AI tool.

## 5. Review Requirements

AI-assisted output **must** be reviewed by a human before it:

- Reaches customers or external stakeholders (including draft replies, proposals, contracts)
- Is used in product, marketing, or public-facing materials
- Informs business decisions with material impact (hiring, pricing, legal positions)
- Is stored as organizational knowledge, policy, or procedure
- Is fed into production systems or automation pipelines

The reviewing person must understand the content, verify critical claims, and take responsibility for its accuracy. For a small team, this typically means the author's manager or a designated peer reviewer.

> **Minimum standard:** At minimum, the author should read the output, check it for obvious errors or hallucinations, and confirm it aligns with the intended message.

## 6. Escalation Triggers

Escalate to your manager and the security owner immediately if:

- An AI tool produces output that appears fabricated, misleading, or harmful
- A data exposure or confidentiality breach is suspected
- An AI tool vendor experiences a security incident or data breach
- The tool is used for a use case not covered by this policy

## 7. AI Tools Approved for Use

| Tool Category | Approved Tools |
|---------------|----------------|
| Writing & Editing | ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Notion AI, Microsoft Copilot |
| Research & Analysis | Perplexity, Gemini, ChatGPT |
| Coding | GitHub Copilot, Cursor, Claude Code |
| Internal Ops | Notion AI, Microsoft Copilot, Google Gemini |

> **Note:** This list is reviewed quarterly. New tools require security owner approval before use.

## 8. Training & Attestation

All team members must:

1. Read this policy
2. Complete the training acknowledgment form
3. Report any policy questions or concerns to their manager

## 9. Exceptions

Any exception to this policy requires written approval from your manager and the security owner.
