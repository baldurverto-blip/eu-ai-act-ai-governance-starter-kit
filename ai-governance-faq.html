AI Governance FAQ

AI Governance for EU Small Business | Templates & EU AI Act Prep

Common questions about AI governance templates, EU AI Act preparation, AI policy documents, and systems registers for EU teams using ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, and similar tools.

What is an AI governance template and do I need one?

An AI governance template is a structured document that helps your team document who uses AI tools, what data those tools access, how output is reviewed, and what happens when something goes wrong. If your team uses ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, or similar tools for business work, you need a basic governance structure—even if you're not legally required to yet.

How does the EU AI Act affect small EU businesses?

The EU AI Act creates obligations for businesses that deploy AI systems, with stricter requirements for "high-risk" AI applications. Most small teams using ordinary AI tools (drafting, summarization, internal assistance) fall under general-purpose AI rules, not high-risk. However, you still need documentation showing what AI systems you use, who owns them, and how you control them—this is where governance templates help.

What is an AI systems register and why does it matter?

An AI systems register is a living inventory of every AI tool your team uses, including the business purpose, data categories involved, named owner, review frequency, and existing controls. It matters because you can't govern what you don't track, and regulators (or customers asking about AI governance) will want to see this list.

Do I need a formal AI policy for my small team?

You need something that defines acceptable AI use, confidentiality expectations, review requirements, and escalation paths—even if it's a simple one-page document. A formal policy becomes essential when multiple people use AI tools, when AI touches customer data, or when you need to show governance discipline to clients or partners.

What is a human review SOP for AI output?

A human review SOP (Standard Operating Procedure) defines which AI-assisted outputs require human review before reaching customers, staff, or important decisions. It covers what triggers review, who performs it, what to check for, and when to escalate. It's the practical control that shows you've moved beyond "trusting AI blindly."

How do I track AI incidents?

You track AI incidents through a dedicated log that captures what happened (hallucination, misuse, data exposure, policy breach), when it occurred, who was involved, root cause, and remediation actions. This matters both for operational improvement and for demonstrating governance discipline if regulators or customers ask.

What should be in a vendor checklist for AI tools?

A vendor checklist covers data handling (where data goes, retention policies), security (encryption, access controls), compliance (what certifications the vendor holds), transparency (model cards, training data disclosure), and support (SLA, incident reporting). Use it before adopting any new AI tool or during annual vendor reviews.

Can I use these governance templates in Google Docs or Notion?

Yes. The templates are provided in markdown and plain text formats designed for easy import into Google Docs, Word, Notion, Excel, or Sheets. The value is in the structure and content, not in a proprietary platform.

Is the AI governance kit enough for EU AI Act compliance?

No. The kit provides documentation and governance templates that support your EU AI Act preparation, but it does not constitute legal compliance. High-risk AI deployments, biometric systems, employment scoring, and other regulated use cases require dedicated legal and technical assessment.

How long does it take to set up AI governance with these templates?

Most teams can adapt the templates, name owners, and create a first-pass AI systems register within a single week. The policy and SOP may take slightly longer depending on your approval process, but the kit eliminates the blank-page problem that usually delays this work.
